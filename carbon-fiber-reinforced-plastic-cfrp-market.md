<p><p>Carbon Fiber-reinforced Plastic (CFRP) is a composite material known for its high strength-to-weight ratio and stiffness. The market can be categorized into various types based on the precursor used for carbon fibers. PAN-based CFRP is derived from polyacrylonitrile, offering superior mechanical properties. Pitch-based CFRP, sourced from petroleum pitch, is known for its thermal stability. Rayon-based CFRP, made from regenerated cellulose, provides a cost-effective option with moderate performance. Other types may include innovative or proprietary fiber blends, catering to specific applications.</p></p>
