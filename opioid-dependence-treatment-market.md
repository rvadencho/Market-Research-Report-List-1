<p><p>The opioid dependence treatment market comprises three main types: Naloxone, Buprenorphine, and Methadone. Naloxone is primarily used to reverse opioid overdoses, providing immediate intervention. Buprenorphine is a partial opioid agonist that helps reduce cravings and withdrawal symptoms, often utilized in maintenance therapy for long-term recovery. Methadone, a full agonist, is used in structured treatment programs to alleviate withdrawal and cravings, allowing patients to stabilize and reintegrate into society. Together, these treatments address varying levels of opioid dependence.</p></p>
