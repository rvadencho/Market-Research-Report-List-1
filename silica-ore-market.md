<p><ul><li>Quartz</li><li>Tridymite</li><li>Cristobalite</li><li>Coesite</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The silica ore market encompasses various types, including quartz, tridymite, cristobalite, and coesite, each characterized by distinct crystallographic forms of silicon dioxide. Quartz is the most abundant and widely used, primarily in glass and ceramics. Tridymite and cristobalite, both high-temperature polymorphs, find applications in specialized industrial processes. Coesite, a rare form formed under extreme pressure, is valuable for high-tech applications. This diversity in silica types drives market demand across industries such as construction, manufacturing, and technology.</p></p>
